What is Hiragana?

Hiragana is one of the three writing systems used in Japanese. (The other two are Katakana and Kanji‚Äîbut don't worry about those yet.)

Think of Hiragana as the "basic alphabet" of Japanese. It's used for:

  • Japanese words that don't use Kanji
  • Grammar particles (small words that connect sentences)
  • Children's books and beginner materials

Every Japanese person learns Hiragana first, usually around age 5 or 6. That means if you're learning Hiragana now, you're following the same path native speakers took.

Hiragana has 46 basic characters. But today, we're only learning 5.

Why Hiragana Comes First

Before you can speak Japanese fluently, you need to read Japanese.

English speakers can get away with learning "romaji" (Japanese words written in English letters), but that creates bad habits. You end up guessing at pronunciation and struggling later when you see real Japanese text.

By learning Hiragana from Day 1, you're learning the real language‚Äîthe way Japanese people actually write and read.

Don't worry if the characters look unfamiliar. In a few days, they'll feel as natural as A, B, C.
